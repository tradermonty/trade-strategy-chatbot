# Dollar Strength Investment Strategy - ドル高進行時の投資戦略

## Overview / 概要

When the US dollar strengthens, it creates specific opportunities and challenges for investors. This guide provides comprehensive strategies for navigating a strong dollar environment.

ドル高局面では、投資家にとって特有の機会と課題が生まれます。本ガイドでは、強いドル環境をナビゲートするための包括的な戦略を提供します。

## Key Indicators of Dollar Strength / ドル高の主要指標

### DXY (Dollar Index) Analysis
- **Strong Dollar**: DXY above 100-105
- **Very Strong Dollar**: DXY above 110
- **Historical Context**: Compare current levels to 5-year and 10-year averages

### Currency Pairs to Monitor
- **EUR/USD**: Below 1.05 indicates dollar strength
- **USD/JPY**: Above 140-145 indicates dollar strength vs yen
- **GBP/USD**: Below 1.20 indicates dollar strength vs pound

## Impact on Different Asset Classes / 資産クラスへの影響

### 1. US Equities / 米国株式

#### Winners in Strong Dollar Environment
- **Domestic-Focused Companies**
  - Retailers with primarily US operations (WMT, TGT, HD)
  - Regional banks (BAC, JPM regional operations)
  - US utilities (NEE, DUK, SO)
  - Healthcare providers (UNH, CVS, HCA)

#### Losers in Strong Dollar Environment
- **Multinational Corporations**
  - Technology giants with high overseas revenue (AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL)
  - Industrial exporters (CAT, BA, MMM)
  - Consumer brands with global presence (KO, PEP, MCD)

### 2. Sector Rotation Strategy / セクター・ローテーション戦略

#### Overweight Sectors
1. **Financials**
   - Banks benefit from higher interest rates often accompanying dollar strength
   - Insurance companies with domestic focus
   - Regional banks over international banks

2. **Real Estate (Domestic REITs)**
   - Residential REITs (AVB, EQR, MAA)
   - Healthcare REITs (WELL, VTR)
   - Self-storage REITs (PSA, EXR)

3. **Utilities**
   - Stable, domestic revenue streams
   - Defensive characteristics
   - Dividend yields become more attractive

#### Underweight Sectors
1. **Technology**
   - High international revenue exposure
   - Translation losses from foreign earnings

2. **Materials & Commodities**
   - Commodities priced in dollars become expensive globally
   - Reduced international demand

3. **Energy**
   - Oil prices often inverse to dollar strength
   - International operations face headwinds

## Specific Trading Strategies / 具体的なトレード戦略

### 1. Currency Hedging Strategies
- **Hedged International ETFs**
  - HEDJ (Europe Hedged Equity)
  - DXJ (Japan Hedged Equity)
  - DBEF (Developed Markets ex-US Hedged)

### 2. Options Strategies
- **Protective Puts** on multinational tech stocks
- **Covered Calls** on domestic-focused dividend stocks
- **Bull Put Spreads** on financial sector ETFs (XLF)

### 3. Pair Trading Opportunities
- **Long**: Domestic retailers (WMT) vs **Short**: Exporters (CAT)
- **Long**: Regional banks (RF) vs **Short**: International banks (C)
- **Long**: Utilities (XLU) vs **Short**: Technology (XLK)

## Fixed Income Considerations / 債券投資の考慮事項

### US Treasury Bonds
- Strong dollar often accompanies higher yields
- Short-duration bonds preferable in rising rate environment
- Consider Treasury Bills (1-3 month) for cash management

### Corporate Bonds
- Focus on domestic issuers with limited FX exposure
- Investment-grade bonds of US-focused companies
- Avoid emerging market debt

## International Investment Adjustments / 国際投資の調整

### Emerging Markets
- **Challenges**: Dollar-denominated debt becomes expensive
- **Opportunities**: Selectively invest after significant corrections
- **Focus**: Countries with strong fundamentals and low dollar debt

### Developed Markets
- European equities face translation losses
- Japanese equities may benefit if BoJ maintains easy policy
- Consider currency-hedged exposures

## Risk Management in Strong Dollar Environment / リスク管理

### Position Sizing
- Reduce allocation to unhedged international positions
- Increase cash reserves in money market funds
- Maintain 10-20% in defensive, domestic positions

### Stop-Loss Strategies
- Tighter stops on export-dependent stocks
- Wider stops on domestic defensive plays
- Use trailing stops to protect gains

### Diversification Rules
- Maximum 30% in any single sector
- Limit international exposure to 20-30% of portfolio
- Maintain 15-25% in fixed income/cash equivalents

## Timing Considerations / タイミングの考慮

### Entry Points
- Dollar strength often occurs in waves
- DXY pullbacks to 20-day MA can be entry opportunities
- Monitor Fed policy announcements

### Exit Signals
- DXY forming lower highs and lower lows
- Fed signaling policy pivot
- Improvement in global growth outlook

## ETF Portfolio for Strong Dollar / 強いドル環境でのETFポートフォリオ

### Core Holdings (60%)
- **SPY or VOO**: S&P 500 exposure (30%)
- **XLF**: Financial sector (15%)
- **XLU**: Utilities sector (15%)

### Satellite Holdings (30%)
- **HEDJ**: Europe Hedged Equity (10%)
- **DXJ**: Japan Hedged Equity (10%)
- **XLV**: Healthcare sector (10%)

### Cash/Fixed Income (10%)
- **SHY**: 1-3 Year Treasury Bonds
- **SGOV**: 0-3 Month Treasury Bills

## Key Metrics to Monitor / モニタリング指標

### Daily Monitoring
- DXY (Dollar Index)
- 10-Year Treasury Yield
- Major currency pairs

### Weekly Review
- Sector performance vs S&P 500
- International markets performance
- Commodity prices (Gold, Oil)

### Monthly Analysis
- Earnings impact from currency translation
- Trade balance data
- Fed meeting minutes

## Common Mistakes to Avoid / 避けるべき一般的な間違い

1. **Over-exposure to Exporters**: Don't hold large positions in multinational companies without hedging
2. **Ignoring Duration Risk**: Long-term bonds suffer in rising rate environments
3. **Currency Speculation**: Avoid leveraged FX trading unless expert
4. **Neglecting Rebalancing**: Regular rebalancing crucial in trending markets
